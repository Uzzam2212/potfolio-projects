The Great Leader

Muhammad Ali Jinnah was a barrister, politician, and the founder of Pakistan. He served as the leader of the All-India Muslim League from 1913 until Pakistan's independence on 14 August 1947, and then as Pakistan's first Governor-General until his death.

Life Timeline

December 25, 1876

Born in Karachi, British India (now Pakistan) to Jinnahbhai Poonja and Mithibai.

1892

Married Emibai Jinnah at the age of 16 (arranged marriage).

1893

Went to London to study law at Lincoln's Inn.

1896

Became the youngest Indian to be called to the bar in England.

1906

Joined the Indian National Congress, beginning his political career.

1913

Joined the All-India Muslim League while remaining in Congress.

1916

Became president of the Muslim League and worked with Congress leaders for Hindu-Muslim unity.

1929

Proposed his Fourteen Points for Muslim political safeguards.

1934

Became permanent president of the Muslim League.

1940

Presided over the Lahore Resolution which demanded a separate nation for Muslims.

August 14, 1947

Pakistan gained independence and Jinnah became its first Governor-General.

September 11, 1948

Died in Karachi at the age of 71. Buried in a grand mausoleum in Karachi.
